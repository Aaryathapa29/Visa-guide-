# Australia Student Visa (Subclass 500) — Requirements Reference (2026)

**Last updated: 2026-07-16 — always verify on official government sites, as rules change.**

This document summarises current requirements for the Australian Student visa (Subclass 500), based on the Department of Home Affairs (immi.homeaffairs.gov.au / homeaffairs.gov.au) and Study Australia (studyaustralia.gov.au). Figures and rules change frequently — confirm on the official links in the Sources section before relying on them.

---

## Overview and who needs it

The Subclass 500 Student visa lets international students stay in Australia to study full-time in a registered (CRICOS) course. It is required by anyone from outside Australia who wants to undertake eligible study, including:

- Higher education, VET (vocational), and ELICOS (English language) courses
- Primary and secondary school, and secondary school exchange programs
- Pathway/foundation courses, postgraduate research, and non-award courses
- Government-sponsored students

The visa allows you to include eligible family members, remain for the duration of your course (up to a maximum of five years), and travel in and out of Australia. You must first enrol and obtain a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) before applying online through ImmiAccount.

## Visa application charge (fee)

- **From AUD 2,500 per application**, effective **1 July 2026** (increased from AUD 2,000).
- Additional applicants (family members) and a "subsequent temporary application charge" may add to this — verify exact amounts on the official pricing pages.
- Some cohorts (e.g. certain ASEAN passport holders, and applicants for independent ELICOS/Non-Award courses) may pay a different charge plus CPI adjustment — verify on official site.

## Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)

You must enrol in a CRICOS-registered course and obtain a **CoE** from your education provider before you can lodge the visa application. The CoE (issued after you accept your Letter of Offer and pay any required deposit) is a core, mandatory document. Applications from students already in Australia also require a CoE.

## Genuine Student (GS) requirement

- **From 23 March 2024, the Genuine Student (GS) requirement replaced the former Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement.**
- Applications lodged on or after 23 March 2024 are assessed under GS; earlier applications used GTE.
- GS is assessed via a **set of targeted questions within the visa application** that you must answer, covering your circumstances, your ties, your chosen course/provider, and how the course benefits you.
- You must show that **studying in Australia is your primary purpose**. Unlike GTE, GS recognises that genuine students may later develop skills Australia needs and apply for permanent residence — such future intentions do not, by themselves, count against an applicant.

## Financial capacity requirement

- **From 10 May 2024, the amount required for a single/primary student applicant is AUD 29,710** (12 months of living costs). This applies to applications lodged on or after 10 May 2024.
- Additional funds are required for a dependent partner and dependent children — the exact per-dependent amounts should be confirmed on the official site (verify on official site).
- Evidence must show **genuine access to funds** to cover the first 12 months of tuition, travel, and living costs for you and any accompanying family. Acceptable evidence includes savings, loans, income, or sponsorship as specified by Home Affairs.

## Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

You must hold **adequate OSHC for the entire duration of your stay** in Australia, purchased from an approved provider. Cover must be maintained continuously as a visa condition (typically condition 8501).

## English language requirement

- Minimum score for a Student visa: **IELTS 6.0** (or equivalent), increased from the previous 5.5.
- Students taking a **pre-main-course ELICOS**: minimum increased to **IELTS 5.0** (from 4.5).
- Students taking recognised **university foundation/pathway programs**: **IELTS 5.5** (or equivalent).
- Hong Kong and British National (Overseas) passport holders retain settings of IELTS 6.0 with a minimum 5.0 in each component — verify on official site.
- Test results must generally be no more than **one year old** at the date of application. Equivalent tests (TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, Cambridge C1 Advanced, OET) are accepted at equivalent scores. Exemptions may apply for some applicants.

## Required documents

- Valid passport
- Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)
- Genuine Student (GS) responses within the application
- Evidence of financial capacity (funds)
- OSHC policy details
- English language test results (if required)
- Health examination (via My Health Declaration / arranged as required)
- Character documents (e.g. police certificates, if required)
- Additional documents for accompanying family members

## Work rights while studying

- **Maximum 48 hours per fortnight** while your course is in session (in effect since 1 July 2023).
- **Unlimited hours** during scheduled course breaks (when study is not in session).
- Students in a **Masters by Research or Doctoral (PhD)** program have **no fortnightly cap** once the course has started.
- Work rights are governed by visa condition **8105**. Work must not begin before your course starts.

## Visa validity and conditions

- Granted for the length of your course, up to a **maximum of five years**.
- Key conditions include:
  - Maintain enrolment and **satisfactory course attendance and progress**
  - **Maintain OSHC** for the whole stay (condition 8501)
  - **Notify your provider of your residential address within 7 days** of arriving, and of any change within 7 days
  - Observe the **48-hour-per-fortnight** work limit (condition 8105)
  - Do not overstay the visa
- Always check your exact conditions in your grant notice or via **VEVO** (Visa Entitlement Verification Online).

## Recent 2024–2026 policy changes

- **23 March 2024:** Genuine Student (GS) requirement replaced GTE.
- **March 2024:** English language minimums raised (Student visa to IELTS 6.0; pre-course ELICOS to 5.0).
- **10 May 2024:** Financial capacity requirement raised to **AUD 29,710** for a primary applicant.
- **1 July 2023:** Work cap set/re-introduced at **48 hours per fortnight**.
- **1 July 2026:** Visa application charge increased to **from AUD 2,500** (previously AUD 2,000).
