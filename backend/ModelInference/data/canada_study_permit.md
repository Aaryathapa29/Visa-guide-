# Canada Study Permit — Requirements Reference (Current as of 2026)

**Last updated: 2026-07-16 — always verify on official government sites, as rules change.**

This document summarizes current Canada study permit rules based on Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) sources. All amounts are in Canadian dollars (CAD).

## Overview: Who Needs a Study Permit

A study permit is the document that allows a foreign national to study at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada. Most international students need one.

**You generally do NOT need a study permit if:**
- Your program lasts **6 months or less** (you may still need a visitor visa or eTA).
- You are a family/staff member of an accredited foreign representative, a member of a foreign armed force on official duties, or taking certain French-language/settlement courses.

Note: If you study without a permit, you cannot work while studying. Study permit holders must stay enrolled at a DLI and make reasonable, timely progress toward completing their program.

## Designated Learning Institution (DLI) Requirement

Your letter of acceptance must come from a **DLI** — a school approved by a province or territory to host international students. If your acceptance letter is not from a DLI, IRCC will refuse the application. All primary/secondary schools are DLIs; post-secondary schools must be verified on the DLI list.

## Provincial/Territorial Attestation Letter (PAL/TAL)

Introduced in 2024, most study permit applicants must include a **PAL or TAL** from the province/territory where they will study.

- You obtain the PAL/TAL through your school; in most cases you must accept your offer and pay tuition (in part or full) first.
- A PAL/TAL for 2026 applications must be issued **between January 1, 2026 and December 31, 2026**. It must be valid when you apply (not when studies begin).
- **Exemptions (as of January 1, 2026):** Master's and doctoral students at public DLIs no longer need a PAL/TAL. Other exemptions include primary/secondary students and certain permit extensions.

## Proof of Financial Support (Cost of Living)

You must prove you can pay for **tuition + travel + living costs** without working in Canada.

- **Cost-of-living amount (outside Quebec): CAD $22,895** per year for a single applicant, effective **September 1, 2025**. This is separate from tuition and travel.
- IRCC adjusts this amount annually based on Statistics Canada's Low-Income Cut-Off (LICO).
- You must show first-year funds if studies exceed one year (or full-duration funds if less than a year), **plus** first-year tuition and travel costs.
- Amounts for **accompanying family members** are higher — verify the exact current per-person figures on the official site.
- Quebec sets its own financial requirements — verify separately.

## Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) / Fast-Track Status

- The **Student Direct Stream (SDS)** and Nigeria Student Express fast-track programs **ended on November 8, 2024**. All applications are now processed under the **regular study permit stream**.
- There is no longer a fixed "SDS GIC" requirement. A GIC is still accepted as valid proof of financial support under the regular stream. If using a GIC, it should cover the required cost-of-living amount (currently CAD $22,895) — verify on the official site.

## Fees (Current)

- **Study permit processing fee (including extensions): CAD $150** per person.
- **Biometrics fee: CAD $85** per person, or **CAD $170** maximum for a family of 2+ applying together.
- Total for a single applicant requiring biometrics: approximately **CAD $235**.

## Required Documents

- Valid passport/travel document
- **Letter of Acceptance (LOA)** from a DLI
- **PAL/TAL** (unless exempt)
- **Proof of financial support** (bank statements, GIC, loan approval, scholarship, etc.)
- Passport photos and biometrics
- Additional documents as requested (e.g., letter of explanation, medical exam, police certificate). Requirements vary by country — check your local visa office instructions.

## Work Rights While Studying

- **On-campus:** Eligible full-time students can work on campus without a separate work permit (no fixed hourly cap while meeting conditions).
- **Off-campus:** Eligible students may work up to **24 hours per week** during academic sessions (increased from 20 hours in late 2024). Exceeding 24 hours violates permit conditions.
- During **scheduled breaks**, eligible students may work full-time.
- You must be a full-time student in an eligible program (at least 6 months, leading to a degree/diploma/certificate) to work off campus without a work permit.

## Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) Basics

A PGWP lets eligible graduates work in Canada after completing studies at an eligible DLI.

- **Length:** Between 8 months and up to 3 years, generally matching program length (bounded by passport expiry).
- **Master's degree exception:** Graduates of a master's program under 2 years may get a 3-year PGWP (since February 15, 2024).

**Recent eligibility changes (2024–2025):**
- **Language proficiency:** As of November 1, 2024, most applicants must show CLB 7 (university) or CLB 5 (college) level language results.
- **Field-of-study requirement:** Applicants who applied for a study permit on/after November 1, 2024 in **non-degree programs** must have graduated in an eligible field of study tied to labour shortages. Bachelor's, master's, and doctoral graduates remain eligible regardless of field (if they meet language rules).
- The eligible fields list was updated June 25, 2025 (approximately 920 fields eligible). Those who applied before that date keep eligibility if their field was listed when they applied.

## Study Permit Caps (2024–2026)

- **2024:** Issuance target ~485,000.
- **2025:** Target ~437,000 study permits (about 10% lower than 2024); PAL/TAL-required and PAL/TAL-exempt allocations distributed accordingly.
- **2026:** Up to **180,000** study permits expected for PAL/TAL-required applicants; approximately **309,670** application spaces available under the cap for processing. Targets are distributed to provinces/territories by population.
