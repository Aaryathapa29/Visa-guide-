# USA F-1 Student Visa — Factual Reference (2026)

Last updated: 2026-07-16 — always verify on official government sites, as rules change.

This document summarizes current U.S. F-1 (academic) student visa requirements, with brief notes on the M-1 (vocational) visa. It is drawn from official U.S. government sources (see Sources). Fees, screening rules, and interview policies change frequently — confirm figures on the official sites before acting.

## 1. Overview: What the F-1 Visa Is and Who Needs It

The **F-1 visa** is for foreign nationals coming to the United States to pursue full-time academic study at a SEVP-certified school (university, college, high school, private elementary school, seminary, conservatory, or approved language program). The **M-1 visa** is for vocational or other recognized nonacademic programs (e.g., technical/trade schools).

Anyone who is not a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident and intends to study full-time in the U.S. generally needs an F-1 (or M-1) visa. You must first be accepted by an SEVP-certified school, which enrolls you in SEVIS and issues a **Form I-20**.

## 2. SEVIS I-901 Fee and Form I-20

- The school issues **Form I-20** (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status) after entering your record in SEVIS. You need the I-20 to pay the SEVIS fee, apply for the visa, and enter the U.S.
- The **I-901 SEVIS fee is $350** for F-1 and M-1 students. It is paid online at **FMJfee.com** before your visa interview. Keep the payment receipt.

## 3. DS-160 and Visa Application (MRV) Fee

- **Form DS-160** is the online Nonimmigrant Visa Application. Complete it, upload your photo, and print the confirmation page for your interview.
- The **visa application (MRV) processing fee for F, M, and J visas is $185** (in effect since June 17, 2023). It is nonrefundable and paid whether or not the visa is approved. Pay per your local U.S. embassy/consulate instructions.

> Note: Separate from these long-standing fees, recent legislation created new government fees affecting some nonimmigrant visa categories. Verify the exact fees that apply to your case on the official embassy/consulate and travel.state.gov pages before paying.

## 4. Financial Requirements / Proof of Funds

You must demonstrate that you can pay **all educational, living, and travel costs** for your program. There is no single fixed government dollar figure — the required amount reflects the estimated cost of attendance on your **Form I-20**. Bring documents such as bank statements, scholarship/assistantship letters, and sponsor affidavits sufficient to cover at least the first year and show funding for the full program.

## 5. Required Documents for the Interview

- Passport valid at least **six months** beyond your intended U.S. stay (unless a country agreement exempts this).
- **Form I-20** (signed).
- **DS-160** confirmation page.
- **MRV fee** receipt and **I-901 SEVIS fee** receipt.
- One photo (if the DS-160 upload failed).
- Supporting documents: proof of finances, academic records/transcripts/test scores, and evidence of ties to your home country and intent to depart after studies.

Each applicant (including any family members) files a separate application.

## 6. Interview Process and Common Questions

A consular officer interviews you to determine eligibility. Digital fingerprints are usually taken. Officers commonly ask about: your chosen school and program, why you selected it, academic and English ability, how you will fund your studies, and your plans and ties after graduation (nonimmigrant intent). You must satisfy the officer that you are a genuine student who intends to return home.

**Interview waivers:** Some F, M, and academic J applicants may qualify for an interview waiver (e.g., certain renewals or prior visa holders), but consular officers may require an in-person interview at any time. Waiver categories have been tightened in 2025 — verify current eligibility on travel.state.gov.

## 7. Visa Validity and the 30-Day Entry Rule

- A visa lets you **travel to a U.S. port of entry and request admission**; CBP officers make the final admission decision. Admission is typically for **Duration of Status (D/S)** for F-1 (you may stay while maintaining status), rather than a fixed date.
- **Initial F-1/M-1 students may enter the U.S. no earlier than 30 days before the program start date** shown on the I-20. To arrive earlier, you would need a separate visitor (B) visa.
- Do not make final travel plans or buy tickets until your visa is issued.

## 8. Working While Studying

- **On-campus employment:** Up to **20 hours/week** when school is in session; full-time during official breaks. Allowed from the start of status under program rules.
- **Curricular Practical Training (CPT):** Work/internship that is an integral part of the curriculum; authorized by your DSO before you begin. Can be part-time or full-time. **12 months or more of full-time CPT eliminates OPT eligibility** (part-time CPT does not).
- **Optional Practical Training (OPT):** Up to **12 months** total per higher degree level. Pre-completion OPT is capped at **20 hours/week** while school is in session; post-completion OPT may be full-time. Requires a USCIS-approved EAD (Form I-765).
- **STEM OPT extension:** F-1 students with a qualifying bachelor's, master's, or doctoral **STEM** degree may apply for a **24-month extension** of post-completion OPT (with an E-Verify employer), for up to 36 months total.
- M-1 students have more limited practical-training options and generally cannot work off-campus except for authorized post-completion practical training.

## 9. Recent 2024–2026 Policy Changes

- **Social media / expanded vetting (June 2025):** F, M, and J applicants must set **all social media profiles to "public"** to allow comprehensive online vetting. Applicants have provided social media identifiers on the DS-160 since 2019.
- **Interview-waiver tightening (2025):** The Department of State narrowed interview-waiver eligibility during 2025; more applicants may face in-person interviews. Verify current rules on travel.state.gov.
- **Fees:** The MRV fee has been $185 since June 2023; the SEVIS I-901 fee is $350. Newer statutory visa fees may apply to some categories — verify on official sites.
